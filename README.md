## 📖 Introduction

Legal teams often receive a large number of client requests through emails, forms, and phone calls. Manually reviewing every request, identifying the legal category, assessing urgency, and assigning the case to the appropriate team consumes significant time and resources.

The Legal AI Intake & Triage System is designed to streamline this process by providing an intelligent digital intake platform that collects client issues, organizes them into a structured format, and prepares them for efficient review and routing.

---

## 🚨 Problem Statement

Legal operations teams face several challenges:

* Large volumes of incoming legal requests.
* Time-consuming manual categorization of issues.
* Difficulty identifying urgent cases quickly.
* Lack of a centralized system for request management.
* Delays in assigning cases to the appropriate legal experts.

These inefficiencies increase response times and reduce operational effectiveness.

---

## 💡 Our Solution

The Legal AI Intake & Triage System provides a centralized platform where clients can securely submit legal concerns through an intuitive web interface.

The system:

* Collects client information and legal issue descriptions.
* Stores requests in a structured database.
* Provides a dedicated admin dashboard for reviewing submissions.
* Creates a foundation for AI-powered legal classification and prioritization.
* Enables faster intake processing and improved workflow management.

By digitizing and organizing the intake process, legal teams can focus more on case resolution rather than administrative overhead.

## 🔄 System Workflow

Client Submission
↓
React Frontend Form
↓
Express Backend API
↓
Supabase Database
↓
Admin Dashboard
↓
Legal Team Review

### Detailed Workflow

1. Client enters personal information and legal issue details.
2. Frontend validates and submits the request.
3. Backend API receives the request.
4. Request data is stored in Supabase.
5. Admin dashboard retrieves all submitted requests.
6. Legal team reviews and processes cases.
